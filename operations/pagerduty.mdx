Some alerts are urgent enough to warrant immediate attention, such as parts of the site becoming unavailable or large quantities of error pages being served. GOV.UK uses [PagerDuty](https://governmentdigitalservice.pagerduty.com) to escalate alerts to on-call engineers in order:

1. Primary Engineer
2. Secondary Engineer
3. GOV.UK Senior Management Team member (may not be technical)

When an alert triggers PagerDuty, someone on the escalation schedule must acknowledge it, otherwise it will be escalated further.

It's the responsibility of each on-call engineer to keep their contact details up to date in PagerDuty and to confirm they are correctly scheduled in.

## Setting up PagerDuty while on-call

<Warning>
  It is your responsibility to ensure you can receive calls from PagerDuty while on-call, including setting it to bypass any Do Not Disturb features on your phone.
</Warning>

<Note>
  From 23 April 2026, you will not be able to use the PagerDuty app on a personal device unless you have accepted the [GDS BYOD policy](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1CP6VKSh5Q2ZzW7po8ehro2I-C--8Uh4y_9Ps4D46FSA/edit). It is highly recommended to use the PagerDuty app on your work mobile device. See [PagerDuty's instructions for setting up the app](https://support.pagerduty.com/main/docs/mobile-app-settings).
</Note>

### Using the mobile app (recommended)

Using the PagerDuty mobile app on your work device makes it easier to ensure that incident notifications reach you. You can send a test notification by:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your PagerDuty profile">
    Click your picture in PagerDuty to access your profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Contact Information">
    Click the "Contact Information" tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a test">
    Click any of the "test" buttons next to your contact methods to verify notifications are working.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Without the mobile app

If you decide not to use the mobile app, or don't have a work mobile device:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the PagerDuty vCard">
    [Download the PagerDuty vCard](https://support.pagerduty.com/main/docs/notification-phone-numbers) and add it to your phone contacts. Download this periodically as the number PagerDuty uses can change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Allow PagerDuty to bypass Do Not Disturb">
    Follow the instructions for your device to allow that contact to bypass Do Not Disturb features:
    - [Android instructions](https://support.google.com/android/answer/9069335?hl=en-GB#zippy=%2Cset-what-to-block-or-who-can-interrupt-you)
    - [iPhone instructions](https://support.apple.com/en-gb/105112)

    Note that Do Not Disturb may be set automatically based on a schedule — check that PagerDuty calls will still come through.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Creating a schedule override

If you need to make a rota swap or cover for someone at the last minute, use the override feature in PagerDuty.

See the [PagerDuty documentation on how to schedule an override](https://support.pagerduty.com/docs/edit-schedules#create-overrides) for step-by-step instructions.

Once you have arranged a swap, let the Technical On-Call delivery manager know so they can confirm the updated schedule.

## PagerDuty drill

GOV.UK tests PagerDuty every Monday morning at 10am UTC (11am BST) to make sure it can alert engineers.

The expected flow during a drill:

1. The **primary in-hours engineer** receives the PagerDuty alert and escalates it to the secondary
2. The **secondary** escalates it to "SMT escalations"
3. The person on **SMT escalations** resolves the PagerDuty alert to prevent anyone else being phoned

Prometheus fires a constant `Watchdog` alert so that developers can confirm Prometheus is integrated with Alertmanager.

### Triggering the drill manually

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ask Platform Engineering to run the drill">
    Ask someone in the `#platform-engineering` team to run the PagerDuty drill.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Exec onto the Alertmanager pod">
    Log onto the production cluster, then exec onto the Alertmanager pod:

    ```shell
    kubectl exec -it alertmanager-kube-prometheus-stack-alertmanager-0 -n monitoring -- sh
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the drill alert">
    Create the PagerDuty drill alert, updating the `--end` datetime to approximately 5 minutes from now:

    ```shell
    amtool --alertmanager.url=http://localhost:9093 alert add \
      --end 2023-11-03T15:59:00-00:00 \
      alertname="PagerDuty test drill. Developers: escalate this alert. SMT: resolve this alert." \
      severity='page'
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify the alert is firing">
    Query the alerts to confirm it is being triggered:

    ```shell
    amtool --alertmanager.url=http://localhost:9093 alert query \
      'alertname=PagerDuty test drill. Developers: escalate this alert. SMT: resolve'
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Add your PagerDuty rota to Google Calendar

You can sync your PagerDuty on-call schedule with Google Calendar so you can see your shifts alongside the rest of your work.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your on-call schedule">
    Go to [https://governmentdigitalservice.pagerduty.com/my-on-call/month](https://governmentdigitalservice.pagerduty.com/my-on-call/month).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the calendar feed URL">
    On the left side, click "Export Calendar". Right-click on "WebCal Feed" and copy the link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add to Google Calendar">
    In Google Calendar, under "Other calendars", click the plus symbol and select "From URL". Paste the WebCal feed URL and add the calendar.
  </Step>
</Steps>
