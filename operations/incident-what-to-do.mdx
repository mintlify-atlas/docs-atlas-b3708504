This page describes what to do when you are responding to an incident on GOV.UK. The full process is documented in the [GOV.UK Incident Management Google Doc](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1ty12B5eBWB9YSfnD9xY1mr5rtTQxdNxRdmEGgibilN0/edit).

<Info>
  If you are on the on-call rota, you should also prepare beforehand using the [GOV.UK Technical On-Call Support](/operations/on-call) page.
</Info>

## Before an incident happens

Make sure you are prepared before your on-call shift starts:

- Review the [on-call checklist](/operations/on-call#on-call-checklist) to confirm access to all required systems
- Ensure PagerDuty is set up to reach you — see [Using PagerDuty](/operations/pagerduty)
- Know how to post a [Statuspage message](/operations/post-a-statuspage-message)
- Be familiar with the monitoring tools: [Grafana](https://grafana.eks.production.govuk.digital/dashboards), [Prometheus](https://prometheus.eks.production.govuk.digital/query), [Alertmanager](https://alertmanager.eks.production.govuk.digital/#/alerts), and [Logit](/operations/logit)

## Incident response steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Acknowledge the alert">
    When PagerDuty pages you, acknowledge the alert to stop it escalating to the next person on the rota. This signals that someone is actively investigating.

    If you cannot investigate immediately, do not acknowledge — let it escalate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assess severity">
    Determine whether this is a real incident affecting users. Check:

    - Is the GOV.UK homepage accessible?
    - Are publishing tools (Whitehall Publisher, etc.) working?
    - Are email alerts going out?
    - Are Pingdom/Alertmanager alerts firing for known services?

    Use [Grafana dashboards](https://grafana.eks.production.govuk.digital/dashboards) and [Kibana logs](/operations/kibana) to understand the scope of the problem.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Notify the team">
    Post in the `#govuk-incident` Slack channel as soon as you identify a real incident. Include:

    - What is affected
    - What you know so far
    - Whether you need help

    The delivery manager on call should be informed so they can coordinate communications.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post a Statuspage message">
    If publishers or the public are affected, post an update on [status.publishing.service.gov.uk](https://status.publishing.service.gov.uk). See [Posting a Statuspage message](/operations/post-a-statuspage-message) for guidance on what to write.

    Start with an "Investigating" status as soon as you know there is an issue — even before you know the cause.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Investigate and diagnose">
    Work through the problem systematically. Useful tools:

    - **[Grafana](https://grafana.eks.production.govuk.digital/dashboards)** — dashboards for request rates, error rates, and service health
    - **[Kibana via Logit](/operations/logit)** — application and request logs
    - **[Sentry](/operations/sentry)** — application errors grouped by project
    - **[Alertmanager](https://alertmanager.eks.production.govuk.digital/#/alerts)** — currently firing alerts

    Keep the `#govuk-incident` channel updated with what you're finding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Escalate if needed">
    If you can't diagnose or fix the problem on your own, escalate in PagerDuty to bring in the Secondary engineer. Stay online to support them and share what you've already found.

    If neither Primary nor Secondary can resolve it, the final escalation goes to a senior member of GOV.UK.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fix and monitor">
    Once you've identified the fix, apply it and update the Statuspage to "Monitoring" status. Keep watching the dashboards and logs to confirm the issue is resolved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolve and close">
    When you are confident the incident is resolved:

    - Update the Statuspage to "Resolved" status
    - Post a final message in `#govuk-incident`
    - Create an incident report (details in the [GOV.UK Incident Management Google Doc](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1ty12B5eBWB9YSfnD9xY1mr5rtTQxdNxRdmEGgibilN0/edit))
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common incident types

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Site is down or returning errors">
    Check the [GOV.UK homepage](https://www.gov.uk) and Pingdom alerts. Look at Grafana for error rate spikes. Check Kibana for recent errors across applications. Check Kubernetes pod status if you suspect a deployment issue.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Publishing is delayed or broken">
    Check Whitehall and Publishing API logs in Kibana. Look for Alertmanager alerts about overdue publications. Check Sentry for errors in publishing apps. Background job queues may be backed up — check Sidekiq dashboards.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Email alerts not going out">
    This is a `severity: page` alert. Check the email-alert-api application logs in Kibana and Sentry. Verify the Notify dashboard to see if messages are being sent.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="High error rate from Router">
    The RouterErrorRatioTooHigh alert will fire. Check Router logs in Kibana. Investigate whether a specific app is down or throwing 5xx errors by checking Grafana error rate dashboards and Sentry.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Communication guidance

  <Card title="Statuspage" icon="globe" href="/operations/post-a-statuspage-message">
    For publisher-facing and public-facing incidents. Write in plain English — avoid internal acronyms.
  </Card>
  <Card title="#govuk-incident Slack" icon="slack">
    Internal coordination channel. Keep it updated throughout the incident with findings and actions taken.

    For on-call coordination and handovers between Primary and Secondary.
  </Card>
