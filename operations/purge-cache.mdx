---
title: Purge the CDN cache
description: How to purge pages and assets from the Fastly CDN cache, and from the standby CloudFront CDN.
---

[Fastly](https://www.fastly.com/products/cdn) caches HTTP resources — HTML pages, images, and scripts — on `www.gov.uk` and `assets.publishing.service.gov.uk` based on the `Cache-Control` headers served from origin. Most pages are cached for **5 minutes**, so when changes are published it may take up to 5 minutes for users to see them.

<Warning>
  If something was published that should not have been published, consider whether there has been a notifiable data breach and ensure someone is handling the matter before purging the cache.
</Warning>

## Check the cache headers for a URL

Before purging, you can inspect the current cache state of any resource using `curl`:

```sh
curl -sI https://www.gov.uk/vat-rates | grep -Ei 'cache-control|age:|x-cache'
```

Example output:

```
age: 107
cache-control: max-age=300, public,private
x-cache: HIT
x-cache-hits: 1
```

| Header | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `age` | Time in seconds since the resource was cached |
| `cache-control` | Where and how long the resource can be cached |
| `x-cache` | Whether the request was served from cache (`HIT`) or origin (`MISS`) |
| `x-cache-hits` | Number of cache hits at this Fastly [point of presence](https://developer.fastly.com/learning/concepts/pop/) |

## Purge a specific URL from Fastly

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log into Fastly">
    Log into [https://manage.fastly.com/](https://manage.fastly.com/). If you don't have an account, ask your tech lead or a member of the senior tech team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the appropriate service">
    Under [All services](https://manage.fastly.com/services/all), choose the service matching what you need to purge:

    - **Production GOV.UK** — for `www.gov.uk`
    - **Production Assets** — for `assets.publishing.service.gov.uk`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Purge the URL">
    From the **Purge** drop-down button near the top left of the page, choose **Purge URL**.

    Enter the full path of the resource to purge — everything after the hostname in the URL — and click **Purge**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Purge all objects from Fastly

<Warning>
  If possible, check with a member of the senior tech team before doing this in production.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log into Fastly">
    Log into [https://manage.fastly.com/](https://manage.fastly.com/).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the appropriate service">
    Under [All services](https://manage.fastly.com/services/all), choose the service you want to purge entirely.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Purge all">
    From the **Purge** drop-down button, choose **Purge all**. The UI will ask for confirmation before issuing the purge request.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Purge from the standby CloudFront CDN

GOV.UK also has a standby CDN on CloudFront, intended only for use during a Fastly outage. You would normally only need to purge its cache in that scenario.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log into the AWS console">
    ```sh
    gds aws govuk-production-developer -l
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the CloudFront distributions page">
    Navigate to the [CloudFront distributions page](https://us-east-1.console.aws.amazon.com/cloudfront/v4/home?region=eu-west-1#/distributions) and choose the appropriate distribution:

    - **WWW** — for `www.gov.uk`
    - **Assets** — for `assets.publishing.service.gov.uk`
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create an invalidation">
    Select the **Invalidations** tab and click **Create invalidation**.

    Enter the full paths of the resources to purge (everything after the hostname). You can use wildcards (`*`). To purge all objects, enter `/*`.

    Click **Create invalidation**.
  </Step>
</Steps>
