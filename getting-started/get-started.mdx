This guide is for new technical staff — developers and technical architects — joining the GOV.UK programme at [GDS](https://gds.blog.gov.uk/about/). It does not apply to Digital Identity, Digital Services Platforms or other GDS programmes.

<Info>
  If you are having trouble with any step, ask your colleagues in the [#govuk-developers](https://gds.slack.com/archives/CAB4Q3QBW) Slack channel.
</Info>

## Before you start

You will need to know who your tech lead is — some steps require their involvement.

If your team does not have a tech lead, or you are the tech lead, contact the Lead Developer in your area or email the [GOV.UK senior tech team](/getting-started/ask-for-help).

You should have a GDS "developer build" laptop with full admin access. Verify this by running:

```sh
sudo whoami
```

It should prompt for your local account password and print `root`. If you do not have admin access, file a ticket with the IT helpdesk and copy your line manager.

## Setup steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install the Xcode command-line tools">
    Run the following in your terminal:

    ```sh
    xcode-select --install
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Install the Homebrew package manager">
    1. Download the latest `.pkg` from the [Homebrew releases page](https://github.com/Homebrew/brew/releases/).
    1. Double-click the downloaded file and complete the installer steps.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill out your Slack profile">
    You should already have access to Slack. If not, speak to your line manager.

    [Update your Slack profile's title field](https://slack.com/intl/en-gb/help/articles/204092246-Edit-your-profile) to include:

    - your job role
    - the team you are working on
    - the name of your organisation, if you are not directly employed by GDS or Cabinet Office
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up your AWS IAM user account">
    1. [Request an AWS user account](https://request-an-aws-user.digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk/user).
    1. You will receive an email when your account is created.
    1. Follow the instructions in the email to sign into the `gds-users` AWS account for the first time.
    1. [Enable Multi-factor Authentication (MFA)](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/IAM/latest/UserGuide/id_credentials_mfa_enable_virtual.html#enable-virt-mfa-for-iam-user) for your IAM user.

    <Warning>
      You must specify your email address as the MFA device name.
    </Warning>

    If you have a Yubikey, you should [use it as your MFA device for AWS](https://docs.publishing.service.gov.uk/manual/setup-a-yubikey.html#set-up-as-an-mfa-device-for-aws).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up your GitHub account">
    1. [Log into your existing GitHub account](https://github.com/login) or [create a new one](https://github.com/signup).
    1. [Add your GDS email address to your GitHub account](https://docs.github.com/en/account-and-profile/setting-up-and-managing-your-github-user-account/managing-email-preferences/adding-an-email-address-to-your-github-account). This can be in addition to your personal email address.
    1. [Generate an SSH key](https://docs.github.com/en/authentication/connecting-to-github-with-ssh/generating-a-new-ssh-key-and-adding-it-to-the-ssh-agent).
    1. [Add the SSH key to your GitHub account](https://docs.github.com/en/github/authenticating-to-github/adding-a-new-ssh-key-to-your-github-account).
    1. Verify that your key works:

    ```sh
    ssh -T git@github.com
    ```

    1. Configure your name and email for git commits:

    ```sh
    git config --global user.email "your.name@digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk"
    git config --global user.name "Your Name"
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get permissions for AWS, GitHub and other third-party services">
    Permissions to GOV.UK's AWS, GitHub, Fastly and PagerDuty accounts are managed by the [govuk-user-reviewer](https://github.com/alphagov/govuk-user-reviewer) private repository. You won't be able to see this repo until you are added to the alphagov GitHub organisation.

    Ask your tech lead to follow the [instructions in govuk-user-reviewer](https://github.com/alphagov/govuk-user-reviewer#adding-users) to grant you access.

    For [Sentry](https://sentry.io/), your tech lead should manually add you via the Sentry UI. Once added, [sign in using your GDS Google account](https://sentry.io/auth/login/).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Install and configure the GDS CLI">
    <Note>
      You must be a member of the [alphagov GitHub organisation](https://github.com/alphagov/) (see the previous step) before proceeding.
    </Note>

    GOV.UK uses `gds-cli` for AWS access. Follow the [installation and configuration instructions](https://github.com/alphagov/gds-cli) in the repo's README.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect to the GDS VPN">
    <Note>
      This step may not be necessary if you are a contractor. Ask your tech lead.
    </Note>

    If you are outside the office or on GovWiFi, you must connect to the GDS VPN to access some internal Cabinet Office services (such as SOP and the intranet).

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="GDS-issued MacBook">
        Follow the [GDS guidance on how to sign into the GDS VPN using Google credentials](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1O1LmLByDLlKU4F1-3chwS8qddd2WjYQgMaaEgTfK5To/edit).
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)">
        Follow the [VPN guide for BYOD](https://docs.google.com/document/d/150JX1xiWdXY29ahcYUMb05Si-hEAZvtkGAKojT9Rjis/edit#).
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up GOV.UK Docker">
    GOV.UK uses a Docker-based environment for local development called [GOV.UK Docker](https://github.com/alphagov/govuk-docker).

    Follow the [installation instructions in the govuk-docker repository](https://github.com/alphagov/govuk-docker#installation).

    <Tip>
      If you are a frontend developer, you may prefer [alternative approaches to local development](https://docs.publishing.service.gov.uk/manual/local-frontend-development.html) that avoid GOV.UK Docker.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set up tools to use the GOV.UK Kubernetes clusters">
    Follow the [instructions for getting started with the GOV.UK Kubernetes clusters](https://docs.publishing.service.gov.uk/kubernetes/get-started/).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get Signon accounts">
    [Signon](https://github.com/alphagov/signon) controls access to the GOV.UK publishing applications.

    Ask your tech lead to create:

    - a **Superadmin** privileged account in [integration Signon](https://signon.integration.publishing.service.gov.uk/users/invitation/new)
    - a **Normal** account in [production Signon](https://signon.publishing.service.gov.uk/users/invitation/new) with access to the **Release** app only

    Production Signon accounts are automatically copied to the staging environment overnight, so you will have staging access the following day.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get familiar with the Release app">
    [Release](https://release.publishing.service.gov.uk/) is the application used to track deployments and determine which branch or tag is deployed to each environment.

    Your tech lead will have granted you access to the Release app in the previous step.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Talk to your tech lead about other supporting services">
    Depending on your role and team, you may also need access to:

    - **Logit** — for reading application and request logs (new developers typically need access to integration logs)
    - **Zendesk** — for working with user support tickets
    - **Google Analytics** — for analysing trends in user behaviour (most new developers won't need this at first)
    - **Terraform** — for reviewing and applying infrastructure changes

    Your tech lead will know which ones you need and can arrange access.
  </Step>
</Steps>
