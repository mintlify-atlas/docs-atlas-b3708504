title: How to ask for help
description: Who to contact and where to go when you need technical help on GOV.UK.
---

If you and your colleagues cannot resolve a technical issue, problem or question, you should escalate in the following order:

1. Your tech lead (TL)
1. The [#govuk-tech-leads](https://gds.slack.com/channels/govuk-tech-leads) Slack channel
1. The [senior tech team](#contact-senior-tech)

## Contact senior tech

You can ping `@govuk-senior-tech-people` on Slack, or email [govuk-senior-tech-members](https://groups.google.com/a/digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk/g/govuk-senior-tech-members/members).

## Ask the developer communities

### GOV.UK developers ([#govuk-developers](https://gds.slack.com/channels/govuk-developers))

The main developer community channel. Members:

- help each other with specialised knowledge about specific areas of the platform
- support each other with issues deploying changes to GOV.UK
- ensure missions are delivered technically in the best and most appropriate way

### GOV.UK Platform Engineering team ([#govuk-ask-platform-engineering](https://gds.slack.com/channels/govuk-ask-platform-engineering))

The Platform Engineering team can help with:

- managing Kubernetes clusters and base images for running GOV.UK applications
- long-term improvements to the efficiency, reliability and security of GOV.UK
- CI/CD (build, release, rollout) automation
- access control automation, including govuk-user-reviewer
- monitoring and alerting advice
- design reviews and advice to help build applications for reliability, robustness and low maintenance — especially useful at early stages of the software lifecycle
- advice and assistance with changes such as migrating between databases safely and efficiently
